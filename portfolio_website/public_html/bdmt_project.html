Course: “Game Mechanics” (Computer Science 306)
Two Player competitive badminton simulation game

My team created a multiplayer game where players compete against each other in a badminton simulation featuring realistic physics. The goal is make your opponent miss the birdie to score a point. The player can hit the birdie in three different ways (under hit, over hit, and regular hit) with a jump modifier on each of these. We also included three more super modifiers (super hit, super gravity on the birdie, and sprint).

Team
Brendon Sterma, Simone Debrowney, Kyle Siemens, Halle Jackson, Daniel Blanche, with music by Aaron Bueckert

The game has two levels, one standard and the other featuring randomly generated wind that is either an advantage or disadvantage to the players. The players are given a warning before this occurs. The game ends when either the timer runs out or if a player reaches 11 points with a 2-point lead. At the end of the game, the players are presented with a rematch suggestion. The game was developed using Unity 5, C#, and Git. The game is played using two XBOX 360 compatible controllers. GitHub Link
